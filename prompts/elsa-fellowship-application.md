## What problem are you solving?

```
x402 just became the official payment protocol for AI agents under the Linux Foundation, backed by founding members including Coinbase, Solana Foundation, Stripe, Google, Visa, Cloudflare, Amazon, Microsoft, Mastercard, and Shopify. 195+ services are already live across the ecosystem, processing $24M+ in monthly volume. No way to know which agents are paying you, how much they are spending, what their churn risk looks like, which endpoints earn the most, or whether your pricing is right. Service providers cannot optimize what they cannot measure. AI agents cannot track their own earnings or understand their own spending. Investors cannot evaluate which x402 services are worth backing. The protocol is ready and the founding members are in place — but the business layer that turns x402 into a real, functioning economy does not exist.

This matters even more for the agentic economy specifically. Agents are autonomous. They make payment decisions on their own. They need observability into their own spending and earnings, and they need it through programmatic interfaces — not just dashboards built for humans. When an AI agent earns revenue from another agent calling its API, the earning agent has no canonical way to track that revenue, compare it across chains, or optimize its own pricing. The agentic economy is being built, but it has no nervous system.

KyvernLabs Pulse is that nervous system.

We solve this with three primitives:

1. One-line middleware that captures every x402 payment with on-chain verification across every supported chain. Multi-chain by default. Built on real Stellar testnet integration via @stellar/stellar-sdk and Horizon API, with Base, Solana, and every chain x402 supports as first-class citizens.

2. A real-time analytics dashboard with revenue charts, customer breakdowns, endpoint performance, agent personas (Whales, Loyalists, Explorers, At Risk), churn prediction, A/B pricing experiments, and revenue forecasting. Built for service providers but designed to be agent-readable.

3. 17 MCP tools that give AI agents direct, programmatic access to their own revenue and spending data from inside Claude, Cursor, or any MCP-compatible client. This is the layer that makes Pulse usable by agents themselves — not just by the humans who built them. An agent can ask "how much did I earn this week?" or "which customer is most likely to churn?" and get a real answer from real on-chain data.

The result: an agent economy where every participant — human service provider, autonomous agent, investor, or builder — has shared, real-time, on-chain-verified visibility into the money that flows through it.

Without observability, the agent economy cannot grow. We are building the observability.
```
