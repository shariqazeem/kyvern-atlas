## How to read this script

This version is written to **sound like you talking to a friend who asked what Kyvern is**, not like a polished ad. Read it the way you'd actually say it out loud. Contractions, easy pace, natural sentences. The numbers and the "Not a server / Not a heuristic / Consensus" triplet are the only parts that need precision delivery. Everything else just needs to sound like you.

## Reading discipline

- Eye contact with the lens, not the screen.
- ~135 wpm. Slightly slower than the demo. Pauses earn weight.
- Don't end any line with rising intonation. Flat or descending.
- The three pauses inside Beat 3 ("Not a server. Not a heuristic. Consensus.") are the most important beats in the whole pitch. Trust them. Don't rush.
- If you lose your line mid-take, breathe, restart the beat from the top, keep rolling. Editor can splice.

## What changed from the previous version

- "More AI agents on the internet than humans" → "more AI agents online than people." Online is what people say. People is what people say.
- "Spend trillions of dollars autonomously" → "spend trillions of dollars on their own." Autonomously is a written word, not a spoken one.
- "Every one of them holds a private key" → "every single one of them is holding a private key." Present continuous makes the danger feel current, not abstract.
- "I'm building from Pakistan. I've shipped three x402 agent projects on Solana." → joined into one breath: "I'm Shariq Shaukat, building from Pakistan. I've shipped three x402 agent projects on Solana over the last year, so I've watched this problem from the inside." More how you actually talk about yourself.
- "Kyvern is the missing piece I've been preparing to build." → "Kyvern is what I've been waiting to build." Builder speech, not pitch speech.
- "Kyvern wraps every agent in a Squads v4 smart account with a custom Solana policy program that enforces..." (one dense sentence) → broken into "Here's how Kyvern works. Every agent gets a Squads v4 vault with our own policy program wrapped around it. The program enforces..." The conversational bridge ("Here's how Kyvern works") prevents the dense list from sounding rehearsed.
- "On-chain. Before any USDC moves." (two hard fragments) → "All on-chain. Before any USDC moves." The "All" softens the fragment landing without losing the punch.
- "Atlas, our reference agent, has been autonomous..." → "Atlas is our reference agent. It's been running autonomously..." Two natural sentences instead of one with embedded clauses.
- "The product works. Today." → "The product works today." One sentence. Same emphasis. Easier to deliver.
- "I built ParallaxPay, an x402 agent marketplace, last quarter on Solana." → "Last quarter I built ParallaxPay, an x402 agent marketplace on Solana." Time first, the way people actually tell stories.
- "Same chain, no keys." → "Same chain. No keys." Two short sentences read sharper aloud.
- "Mainnet next month." → "We ship to mainnet next month." Complete sentence. Closes the pitch without leaving the camera feeling clipped.
